Description


Margherita pizza is a classic Italian style of pizza defined by its light, fresh profile and reliance on just a few basic toppings. It features a simple dough base baked at high heat until bubbly and lightly charred, topped with a smooth, savory red sauce, melted white cheese, a handful of fresh green herbs, and a light pour of oil.

During an 1889 visit to Naples, a local pizza maker created a custom pie to honor her. He chose ingredients that mirrored the red, white, and green colors of the Italian flag. The Queen loved the creation, securing its place in culinary history.



Ingredients


  • 1 premade pizza dough
  • 1/2 jar pizza sauce usually around 7-8 oz.
  • 4 oz. fresh mozzarella usually half a fresh mozzarella ball
  • 5-10 basil leaves fresh


Step-By-Step Recipe

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees.
  2. Spread the dough into a circular pie crust and place either on a pizza stone or a 12-inch cast iron skillet. Do not form a crust with the dough, the pizza center will lose form and it will be hard to eat.
  3. Spread the pizza sauce onto the dough using a spoon, Leave room on the sides for a “crust” for easy handling.
  4. Add the fresh mozzarella slices on top of the sauce, but do not overlap the cheese. Leave the circles on their own.
  5. Spread out the fresh basil on top
  6. Bake in the oven for 15-20 minutes, or until the cheese starts to turn golden brown.
